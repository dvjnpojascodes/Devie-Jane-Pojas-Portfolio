SIKLISTO: Electric Bike Charging Station Locator App

1. Overview

With greater versatility and a variety of intended usage areas, e-bikes are becoming increasingly popular and a possible alternative mode of transportation in the Philippines. However, despite the growth of e-bike, the few e-bike charging facilities installed in the country. Siklisto is an application that uses the Google Maps Platform Routes to locate the e-bike charging stations in National Capital Region (NCR), provides users information about the station, and allows to add e-bike charging stations.

Role

UI Designer, Prototyping, and Testing

Date Duration: December 2022 - Ongoing

2. Defining the Problem and Goals

2.1 Problems

Currently, there's no application that is focus on e-bike charging stations Results on EV charging stations apps are often for e-cars, which generally charge on Level 2 receptacles, unlike electric bicycles that use Level 1 charging.

Users ask on Facebook to search e-bike charging stations E-bike users in the National Capital Region (NCR) use social media, such as Facebook groups, as a medium to ask for and find e-bike charging stations near their location

Internet search results are not reliable. Similar to EV charging staion apps, search results on interent for e-bike charging station shows e-cars charging stations only.


2.2 Goals

Improve the accessibility and visibility of electric bike charging stations This will help in minimizing the time it takes e-bike users to find charging stations by giving precise locations through the application.

Provide information about the charging station The information provided by the app about charging station address, availability, charging cost aims to help users make informed decisions about where to charge their e-bikes.

3. Wireframes

After identifying the problems and features of the apps, I created wireframes to visualize the overall layout and structure of the app modules, before moving on to designing the user interface. Creating wireframe involved the visual heirarchy and layout of the elements and components to be put on the app.

4. HI-FI UI DESIGN

Visuals

For the user interface, I used different shade of green to provide a fresh visual appeal to user. Moreover, to show how e-bikes offer a environment-friendly alternative mode of transportation. The font used in the application utilized Montserrat for simplicity and Lexend, which are intended to reduce visual stress and improve reading performance.
